A better primary care experience

Direct primary care gives patients more time, easier access, and fewer barriers between questions and answers.

CKM’s direct primary care model is built for people who are tired of rushed visits, confusing insurance friction, and feeling like they need to start from the beginning every time they need help.

What direct primary care means here

Direct primary care is a self-pay model centered on access, continuity, and relationship-based care. Instead of squeezing care into a volume-driven system, the focus stays where it belongs: listening, planning, and following through.

For many patients, that means a more personal experience, more transparency, and fewer delays when something needs attention.

Membership highlights
  • Wellness visits
  • Sick visits
  • Same-day appointments during office hours when available
  • Clear self-pay structure

For busy adults

A direct primary care model works especially well for people who value straightforward communication and want a provider they can actually build trust with.

For patients who want continuity

It is easier to make good decisions when your provider knows your history, your goals, and your patterns over time.

For people frustrated by rushed care

Many patients are not just seeking treatment. They are seeking time, context, and the chance to ask the questions that matter.

For patients who want a medical home

CKM can serve as a trusted first stop for routine care, preventive support, and health concerns that need attention before they become bigger problems.

What is included?

CKM currently presents membership support for same-day appointments during office hours when available, sick visits, and wellness visits. The final site can expand this with clearer expectations and FAQs.

Do I need insurance?

No insurance is needed for care at CKM. The point is simplicity, transparency, and more direct access to your provider.

Is this still real primary care?

Yes. It is primary care delivered through a different payment and access model, with more emphasis on relationship, prevention, and responsiveness.

Who is this best for?

Adults who want self-pay simplicity, easier access, and a provider who takes the time to understand the full picture.
