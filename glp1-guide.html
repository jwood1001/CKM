Know what you are starting before you start

GLP-1 medications can support real weight loss — when you understand how they work and have a plan behind them.

Semaglutide and tirzepatide are effective tools, but the outcomes patients see most often involve more than a prescription. They involve education, realistic expectations, side-effect support, and a provider relationship that helps when things get harder before they get easier.

How It Works

What these medications actually do

GLP-1 receptor agonists slow gastric emptying, reduce appetite signals, and help regulate blood sugar. They do not burn fat directly — they make it easier to eat less consistently over time. Dosing starts low and increases gradually over several weeks so the body can adjust with fewer side effects.

Best Practices

Hydration, protein, and movement

Patients on GLP-1 medication often eat significantly less, which makes protein intake more important, not less. Aim for at least 80–100g of protein daily to protect lean muscle. Staying well-hydrated reduces constipation risk. Gentle movement — especially walking — supports both mood and metabolic health throughout treatment.

Safety

When to call the clinic

Contact CKM if you experience severe nausea or vomiting that prevents eating or drinking, persistent abdominal pain, signs of an allergic reaction, or anything that feels unusual compared to what you were told to expect. Mild nausea in the first few weeks is common. Severe or prolonged symptoms are not something to wait out alone.

What to expect

Common questions in the first 90 days.

What to expect in your first 12 weeks

Most patients start at a low dose — 0.25mg for semaglutide or 2.5mg for tirzepatide — and increase every four weeks. The first few weeks are about tolerating the medication more than seeing dramatic results. Nausea is common early. Progress tends to accelerate between weeks 4 and 12 and varies based on starting point, adherence, and how your metabolism responds.

Managing nausea and constipation

Eat smaller portions more slowly, avoid high-fat meals, and stay well-hydrated. Timing your injection before bed can help with nausea. Constipation is often managed with added fiber, water, and light movement. If either symptom becomes disruptive or does not improve, reach out — dose timing or titration can sometimes be adjusted.

Body composition vs. scale weight

The scale is a limited measure. GLP-1 treatment can shift fat mass significantly while lean muscle stays stable — or it can lead to muscle loss if protein intake and activity are not managed carefully. CKM uses Fit3D body-composition scans to track the actual breakdown, not just the number you see on the scale.
