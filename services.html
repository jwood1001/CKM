Detailed services

Every service should feel clear, credible, and worth choosing.

Patients should not have to guess what a treatment is, whether it is right for them, or what kind of result is realistic. This page gives CKM a place to explain the full service lineup in patient-friendly language that feels polished and medically grounded.

Urgent Care

Fast access when something comes up and you do not want the hassle of traditional urgent care.

CKM's urgent care positioning works best when it is framed as convenient, local, same-day problem-solving for common acute needs rather than a rushed volume-driven experience.

Patients choose this service because they want a trusted provider, a simpler self-pay option, and a place that feels more personal than walking into a generic urgent care center.

What it is: Evaluation and treatment for common acute concerns that need timely attention. Why people choose it: Faster access, more continuity, and less insurance friction. What to expect: A focused visit, practical next steps, and guidance on whether follow-up or additional care is needed. Realistic expectations: Great for many everyday concerns, but not a substitute for emergency care when symptoms are severe.
Red Light Therapy

A supportive wellness add-on for patients who want recovery-focused care as part of a broader plan.

Red light therapy is best described as a complementary wellness tool, not a miracle treatment. It can support recovery-oriented routines and is most credible when presented as one piece of a larger care plan.

What it is: A light-based wellness service used to support recovery-focused routines. Why people choose it: They want a low-friction add-on that fits into a wellness plan. What to expect: A calm, straightforward visit with no dramatic downtime. Realistic expectations: Supportive and complementary, not a stand-alone answer for major health concerns.
Fit3D Body Scanner

Show progress in a way the scale never can.

Fit3D is one of CKM's strongest premium differentiators because it turns vague progress into something visual and measurable. This matters especially for weight-loss patients who need more than a scale number to stay encouraged.

What it is: A body-composition and measurement-tracking tool that helps visualize progress. Why people choose it: They want a clearer picture of change than weight alone can give them. What to expect: A quick scan with measurements and trend tracking over time. Realistic expectations: It improves insight and accountability, but it is a tracking tool rather than a treatment itself.
Microneedling with PDO AfterGlo

Skin-renewal support for texture, tone, and a more refreshed look.

Microneedling should be described as a collagen-supporting treatment that helps improve texture, tone, and overall skin appearance over time. Patients respond well when expectations are realistic and aftercare is explained clearly.

What it is: A professional skin treatment designed to support renewal and improve surface quality. Why people choose it: They want smoother texture, a healthier-looking glow, and a more polished appearance. What to expect: Mild downtime, temporary redness, and a treatment plan that may work best in a series rather than one session. Realistic expectations: Improvements are gradual. Skin quality can look better over time, but it is not an overnight transformation.
Botox Cosmetic

Refined, natural-looking softening of visible lines without sacrificing expression.

Botox should be positioned as consultation-led care for patients who want to look refreshed, not overdone. The strongest message is subtle confidence, not dramatic change.

What it is: An injectable aesthetic treatment used to soften certain expression lines. Why people choose it: They want a smoother, more rested look with a treatment that fits a professional lifestyle. What to expect: A brief visit, a tailored treatment plan, and results that become more noticeable gradually after treatment. Realistic expectations: Results are temporary, maintenance is expected, and the best outcomes look subtle and balanced.
BioTe Hormone Pellets

An option for patients who want provider-guided hormone support with a personalized care plan.

BioTe hormone pellet therapy should be discussed carefully and clearly. Patients want to understand why it may be considered, how it fits into a broader hormone evaluation, and what follow-up looks like.

What it is: A hormone-support option used within a personalized care plan when clinically appropriate. Why people choose it: They are looking for support with symptoms such as energy shifts, body changes, or feeling unlike themselves. What to expect: A discussion of symptoms, evaluation, candidacy review, and follow-up rather than a one-size-fits-all approach. Realistic expectations: Hormone care is individualized. Results and fit depend on symptoms, health history, and provider judgment.
